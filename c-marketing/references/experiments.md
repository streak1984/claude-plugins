# A/B Test Ideas by Page Type

Reference list of proven experiment ideas. Use as inspiration when recommending tests — always adapt to the specific page, audience, and context.

---

## Homepage

### Headlines & Value Prop
- **Benefit vs. feature headline**: Test "Spar 10 timer i uken på rapportering" vs. "Automatisert rapporteringsverktøy"
- **Specific vs. generic**: Test concrete numbers/outcomes vs. broad claims
- **Customer-centric vs. product-centric**: "Du får..." vs. "Vi tilbyr..."
- **Question headline**: Test a question that speaks to the visitor's pain vs. a statement

### Navigation & Layout
- **Simplified navigation**: Test removing menu items to reduce choice paralysis
- **Hero image vs. no image**: Some products convert better with a clean text-focused hero
- **Video hero vs. static**: Test auto-play product demo vs. static screenshot
- **Single CTA vs. multiple paths**: One clear next step vs. segment-specific options

### Social Proof
- **Logo bar position**: Test above fold vs. below fold
- **Specific numbers**: "1 247 norske bedrifter" vs. "Tusenvis av kunder"
- **Testimonial with photo vs. logo only**: Named person with headshot builds more trust
- **Industry-specific proof**: Show proof relevant to the visitor's segment

## Pricing Page

### Price Presentation
- **Annual vs. monthly default**: Test which toggle position is pre-selected
- **Per-user vs. flat pricing display**: Frame depends on perceived value
- **Highlight savings**: "Spar 2 400 kr/år" badge on annual plan
- **Anchor pricing**: Show enterprise/premium first to make mid-tier feel reasonable

### Plan Layout
- **3 plans vs. 2**: Sometimes removing the cheapest plan increases mid-tier conversions
- **Recommended badge**: Test different plans as "Most popular" / "Anbefalt"
- **Feature comparison toggle**: Collapsed vs. expanded comparison table
- **Plan naming**: Descriptive names ("For team", "For bedrift") vs. tier names ("Pro", "Enterprise")

### Reducing Friction
- **FAQ on pricing page**: Test adding vs. not having FAQ section
- **Money-back guarantee badge**: Near CTA, test with/without
- **Chat widget on pricing**: Test live chat for pricing questions
- **Comparison with competitors**: Bold move — test a "vs. alternatives" section

## Landing Page

### Headline & Copy
- **Short vs. long landing page**: Test minimal (headline + CTA + proof) vs. detailed
- **Negative vs. positive framing**: "Slutt å kaste bort tid på..." vs. "Få mer tid til..."
- **Testimonial as headline**: Use a customer quote as the main headline
- **Specificity**: Test exact numbers ("37% raskere") vs. general claims ("Mye raskere")

### Form & CTA
- **Form length**: Test 3 fields vs. 5 fields vs. 7 fields
- **Multi-step form**: Break long forms into 2-3 steps with progress indicator
- **CTA color**: Test high-contrast vs. brand-consistent button color
- **CTA copy**: "Start gratis" vs. "Prøv gratis i 14 dager" vs. "Se hvordan det fungerer"
- **Button size**: Test larger CTA button — especially on mobile
- **Microcopy under CTA**: "Ingen kredittkort nødvendig" / "2 minutter å sette opp"

### Trust & Proof
- **Video testimonial vs. text**: Test embedded customer video vs. written quote
- **Before/after**: Show the transformation with specific metrics
- **Risk reversal near CTA**: Guarantee, free trial, cancel anytime — positioned next to the button
- **Security badges**: Test adding payment/security icons near form submit

### Layout
- **Remove navigation**: Test landing page with no main menu (fewer exit paths)
- **Sticky CTA**: Test fixed CTA button that follows scroll
- **Content order**: Test proof → offer → CTA vs. offer → proof → CTA
- **Exit intent popup**: Test a softer offer (guide, checklist) when visitor moves to leave

## Blog / Content Pages

### Lead Capture
- **Inline CTA vs. sidebar**: Test CTA placement within content vs. sidebar widget
- **Content upgrade**: Test topic-specific lead magnet vs. generic newsletter signup
- **Exit intent vs. scroll trigger**: Test popup timing — 50% scroll vs. exit intent
- **Embedded form vs. link to landing page**: Test friction of staying on page vs. dedicated conversion page

### Content Format
- **Table of contents**: Test adding a clickable TOC for long articles
- **TL;DR section**: Test adding a summary box at the top
- **Progressive disclosure**: Test expandable sections for detailed content
- **Related content placement**: Bottom of article vs. inline between sections

### Engagement
- **Author bio with CTA**: Test author section that includes a relevant CTA
- **Social share buttons**: Test prominent placement vs. hidden/removed
- **Comment section**: Test with comments vs. without (comments can increase time on page but distract from CTA)
- **Reading time estimate**: Test showing estimated read time in header

## Demo Request / Contact Page

### Form Optimization
- **Number of fields**: Test minimal (name, email) vs. qualified (add company, role, size)
- **Required vs. optional fields**: Mark most fields optional — test impact on completion
- **Calendar embed**: Test inline Calendly vs. "We'll contact you" form
- **Phone number field**: Test removing it (highest-friction field)

### Copy & Context
- **"Book a demo" vs. "See it in action"**: Test CTA language
- **What to expect section**: "30-minutters gjennomgang, ingen forpliktelser" — test adding this
- **Team photo**: Test showing who they'll talk to
- **Response time promise**: "Vi svarer innen 2 timer" — test adding urgency/reliability

### Social Proof
- **Customer logos near form**: Test adding logo bar directly above/below form
- **Testimonial from similar company**: "Vi er 15 ansatte og fikk resultater på 2 uker"
- **Demo recording preview**: Test showing a 30-second clip of the demo experience

## General Principles

When designing tests:

1. **One variable per test** — change one element to isolate impact
2. **Hypothesis first** — "We believe [change] will [impact] because [reason]"
3. **Minimum sample size** — don't call tests early. Use a calculator for statistical significance
4. **Test big changes first** — headline rewrites before button color changes
5. **Document everything** — log tests, results, and learnings regardless of outcome
6. **Consider segments** — a test might win overall but lose for your best customers
7. **Mobile vs. desktop** — test results often differ by device. Segment results.
