# Email Sequence Templates

Reference templates for common sequence types. Use as starting points — adapt timing and count to the specific audience and product.

---

## Welcome Sequence (7 emails)

New subscribers or trial signups. Goal: activate and build trust fast.

| # | Delay | Job | Notes |
|---|-------|-----|-------|
| 1 | Immediate | Deliver promised value + set expectations | What they'll get, how often. Deliver lead magnet if applicable. |
| 2 | +1 day | Quick win | One actionable tip they can use today. Build momentum. |
| 3 | +2 days | Your story / why this exists | Personal connection. Why you built this, what you believe. |
| 4 | +3 days | Social proof | Case study, testimonial, or results. Show others like them succeeding. |
| 5 | +5 days | Overcome main objection | Address the #1 reason people don't act. |
| 6 | +7 days | Deep value | Best resource, guide, or insight. This is the "wow" email. |
| 7 | +10 days | Soft CTA | Invitation to next step (upgrade, book call, join community). Low pressure. |

**Copy patterns:**
- Email 1: "Velkommen! Her er [verdi]. De neste ukene sender jeg deg [hva]. Forvent en e-post [frekvens]."
- Email 3: Keep story under 300 words. One turning point, one lesson.
- Email 7: Frame as invitation, not hard sell. "Hvis du er klar for..."

## Nurture Sequence (8 emails)

Leads who showed interest but haven't converted. Goal: educate and build enough trust for the ask.

| # | Delay | Job | Notes |
|---|-------|-----|-------|
| 1 | Immediate | Restate the problem they have | Show you understand their situation deeply. |
| 2 | +3 days | Teach framework/mental model | Give them a new way to think about the problem. |
| 3 | +5 days | Case study | Someone like them who solved it. Specific numbers. |
| 4 | +7 days | Common mistakes | What most people get wrong. Position yourself as expert. |
| 5 | +10 days | Quick win | Actionable tip they can implement today. |
| 6 | +14 days | Deeper insight | The "aha" moment. Content that changes their perspective. |
| 7 | +17 days | FAQ / objection handling | Address top 3 reasons people hesitate. |
| 8 | +21 days | The ask | Clear, confident CTA. They have all the context they need. |

**Copy patterns:**
- Email 1: Lead with their pain, not your solution. "Du kjenner kanskje igjen dette: [specific situation]."
- Email 4: "De fleste gjør X. Det fungerer ikke fordi Y. Gjør Z i stedet."
- Email 8: Direct but respectful. Reference what you've covered. "Over de siste ukene har jeg vist deg [verdi]. Nå er spørsmålet: [CTA]."

## Re-engagement Sequence (4 emails)

Inactive subscribers (30-90 days no opens/clicks). Goal: re-activate or clean the list.

| # | Delay | Job | Notes |
|---|-------|-----|-------|
| 1 | Immediate | "We miss you" + best content | Lead with value, not guilt. Share your best-performing piece. |
| 2 | +4 days | What's new | Changes, improvements, new content since they disengaged. |
| 3 | +7 days | Direct ask | "Vil du fortsatt høre fra oss?" with clear yes/no. |
| 4 | +14 days | Last chance | Final email before unsubscribe. Clean break. |

**Copy patterns:**
- Email 1: Don't say "we noticed you haven't opened." Lead with value: "Denne artikkelen ble den mest leste i [måned]. Har du sett den?"
- Email 3: Honest and brief. "Jeg vil ikke fylle innboksen din med ting du ikke vil ha. Klikk her for å bli, eller gjør ingenting så fjerner vi deg."
- Email 4: Short. 3-4 sentences max. "Siste sjanse" framing. Respect their decision either way.

**Timing note:** Check email tool for automatic sunset/cleanup features before building manually.

## Onboarding Sequence (7 emails)

New customers/users after purchase or signup. Goal: drive adoption and reduce churn.

| # | Delay | Job | Notes |
|---|-------|-----|-------|
| 1 | Immediate | Welcome + first step | One action. Not a feature dump. "Gjør dette først." |
| 2 | +1 day | Second step + why it matters | Build on first step. Show the benefit they'll unlock. |
| 3 | +3 days | "Aha moment" push | Guide them to the core value moment. This is the make-or-break email. |
| 4 | +5 days | Pro tip | Power user feature or shortcut. Make them feel smart. |
| 5 | +7 days | Social proof | How others use the product. Inspire use cases they haven't considered. |
| 6 | +14 days | Check-in | "Hvordan går det?" Personal, brief. Offer help. |
| 7 | +21 days | Next level | Upsell or deeper feature. Only if they've engaged with earlier emails. |

**Copy patterns:**
- Email 1: Skip "welcome aboard" platitudes. Go straight to "Her er det første du bør gjøre: [specific action]."
- Email 3: This is the most important email. Whatever your product's "aha moment" is, drive them there.
- Email 6: Genuine check-in, not fake friendliness. Ask a specific question they can reply to.

**Segmentation:** Branch after email 3 — active users get emails 4-7, inactive users get a separate help/support sequence.

## Launch Sequence (5-7 emails)

Product launch, course launch, or event. Goal: build anticipation and convert during a window.

| # | Delay | Job | Notes |
|---|-------|-----|-------|
| 1 | -7 days | Tease | Hint at what's coming. Build curiosity without revealing everything. |
| 2 | -3 days | Behind the scenes | Why you built this, the story, the problem it solves. |
| 3 | -1 day | Anticipation | "I morgen." Details, pricing if applicable, what to expect. |
| 4 | Launch day | The offer | Full details. Clear CTA. Urgency if genuine (limited spots, early bird). |
| 5 | +1 day | Social proof | Early results, testimonials, "X people have already..." |
| 6 | +3 days | FAQ / objections | Address hesitations. For those on the fence. |
| 7 | Close day | Last chance | Deadline. Short, direct. "Tilbudet stenger i kveld kl. 23:59." |

**Copy patterns:**
- Email 1: Don't oversell the tease. "Jeg har jobbet med noe de siste månedene. Mer neste uke."
- Email 4: Be direct. Lead with the offer, then support with proof and details.
- Email 7: Short. Under 100 words. Pure urgency. No new information needed.

**Important:** Only use urgency if it's real. Fake deadlines destroy trust. If there's no deadline, drop emails 6-7 and extend the value-based emails.

## Email Length Guidelines

| Email Type | Word Count | When to Use |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Transactional / trigger | 50-125 words | Confirmations, reminders, status updates |
| Educational / value | 150-300 words | Tips, insights, frameworks, quick wins |
| Story-driven / case study | 300-500 words | Origin stories, case studies, deep dives |
| Launch / sales | 200-400 words | Offers, launches, time-sensitive CTAs |
| Re-engagement | 50-150 words | Win-backs, check-ins, sunset emails |

**Rule of thumb:** If the email serves one job well, it's the right length. Cut until cutting would remove meaning.
